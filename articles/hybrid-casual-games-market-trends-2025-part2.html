Hybrid Casual Games Market Trends 2025 (Part 2)

Sep 7, 2025

This is the continuation of our Hybrid Casual Games Market Trends 2025 (Part 1), where we covered rankings #1–#6. In this part, we dive into games ranked #7–#10.

2025 Q2 Hybrid Casual Games Ranking (Part 2: #7–#10)

7
Screw Sort Puzzle™ – Cube Jam 3D (BitEpoch)
$4.7M Revenue | 3.1M Downloads

Growth: $4.8M IAP in Q2 (+258% QoQ), breaking into the Top 10 in the crowded screw puzzle genre.

Design: Alternates 2D and 3D levels for variety, blending hybrid casual systems like pre-level boosters and lightweight character collection.

Monetization: Piggy bank expanded to $1.99–$14.99 tiers, retry discounts raised from $4.99→$5.99, boosting ARPPU.

LiveOps: Daily/monthly leaderboards, weekly quests, lucky spins, milestone and timed events sustain engagement and drive spending.

Market alignment: “Obstacle Challenge” event mirrors the Lava Quest trend (adopted by 41% of top casual games in 2025).

8
Tower War – Tactical Conquest (SayGames)
$3.5M Revenue | 9.5M Downloads

Launched in 2021, this RTS hybrid balances simple controls with unit progression and tactical depth.

Operations: Regular PvP/PvE competitions and variant events maintain freshness.

Monetization: Reward system tied to milestone progression and shop exchanges, blending rewarded ads with battle passes and bundles.

9
Epic Plane Evolution (Voodoo)
$3.5M Revenue | 8.6M Downloads

Performance: +265% QoQ, driven by U.S. market growth two years after launch.

Transformation: Shifted from ad-driven hyper-casual to IAP-first hybrid model with “plane ticket” mechanic limiting play frequency.

Pricing strategy: Gems and bundles repriced upward (10 gems $1.99→$4.99, 100 gems $9.99→$24.99, ad removal $2.00→$6.99).

Impact: ARPPU lifted to ~$10, well above hybrid casual averages, fueling its climb in the grossing charts.

10
Knit Out (Rollic)
$3.4M Revenue | 1.5M Downloads

Context: Rollic’s third Top 10 title this quarter, leveraging the knitting theme trend (280+ new releases Jan–Jul 2025).

Launch timing: Released in Jan 2025 ahead of trend peak, amplified by smart ASO during April–May surge.

Gameplay: Untangle ropes by matching spools, with a smooth difficulty curve sustaining retention.

Monetization: New “Spool Pass” contributes 5–7% monthly IAP, combined with energy gating, consumables, and end-of-level offers.

Core Insights & Conclusion

  • Market concentration: Rollic (3 titles) and Voodoo (2 titles) dominate half of the Top 10, same as Q1 2025.
  • Barriers & fluidity: Entry is tougher, but the Top 10 remains dynamic with opportunities for challengers.
  • Case examples: Hole People & Knit Out follow Color Block Jam’s winning formula; Epic Plane Evolution showcases hyper-to-hybrid transition; Screw Sort Puzzle™ proves mid-size studios can break through with disciplined monetization.

Hybrid casual growth stems not just from borrowing proven mechanics but also from smart UA strategies. Sticky core loops, viral themes, and optimized monetization lower CPI and unlock scalable potential.

By Q2 2025, hybrid casual games further consolidated their influence with no slowdown in sight. For developers, hybrid casual is no longer optional—it is the growth playbook. The earlier teams adapt, the more likely they are to lead rather than chase.
